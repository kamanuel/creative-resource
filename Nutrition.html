Food/Nutrition 

Patients seeking groceries can take advantage of The Corner Store and Pantry, located inside the clinic. The pantry includes groceries provided by food gatherers, while the store has a variety of items, including baby clothes, household items, and beauty products.  


Store Hours: 

Monday: 9-12 p.m., 1-4:45 p.m.

Tuesday: 9-12 p.m., 3-4:45 p.m.

Wednesday: 1-4:45 p.m.

Thursday: 9-12 p.m., 1-4:45 p.m.

Friday: 9-12 p.m., 1-4:45 p.m.


There are also several resources to obtain groceries in the community: 

  • Maggie’s Marketplace
    • Located inside the Ypsilanti Health Center (YHC) 
  • Packard Health Food Pantries
    • Ypsilanti Pantry at 200 Arnet St
    • Carpenter Pantry at 2650 Carpenter Rd
    • West Pantry at 1915 Pauline Blvd 
  • The Love Stand 
    • 315 N. River Street, Ypsilanti
    • Open 24/7 in front of the house; No ID or proof of income required 
  • Hope Clinic 
    • 518 Harriet Street, Ypsilanti 
    • Offer access to a food pantry by appointment and a walk-up food stand
  • Women, Infants & Children (WIC) 
    • Supplemental nutrition program available to pregnant and postpartum women and children up to 5 years of age. In addition to food packages they also provide nutrition counseling and breastfeeding support. 
  • SNAP/Bridge 
    • See social work for additional guidance on how to utilize this resource 
  • Prescription for Health 
    • A program through the Washtenaw County Health Department that connects patients to local farms markets through their medical clinics, including Corner. Erolled patients receive tokens that they are able to spend on fruits and vegetables at participating local farmers market. 


Nutrition 

Nutrition counseling is available in the clinic as an internal referral. Our nutritionists can help with weight management and metabolic disorders

Subject: Nutrition (diabetes) 

Description: 21 yo with BMI of 40 and prediabetes (A1c 6.1)
